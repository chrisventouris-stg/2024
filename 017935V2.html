  Experience is... a holiday as unique as you
 
Whether it’s an age-old festival or a modern-day fiesta, one of the best ways to experience the unique culture of a place is to join in with local celebrations. From the dazzling costumes and superb street food of Mardi Gras, to Japan’s sweet-smelling cherry blossoms, there are countless events to choose from. Scroll down for inspiration.
Along with a huge range of enriching destinations, there’s a whole host of ways to explore them with Saga – from escorted tours and hosted hotel stays to independent getaways customisable to suit you.
Explore the collection
 
 
Festivals in Madeira
The Madeira Flower Festival is held every spring – a nod to the island’s rich botanical heritage. Expect parade floats cloaked in blooms, stunning floral carpets, and live music. The capital, Funchal, hosts the main event, and you can take part, or just enjoy the view from the city’s surrounding hotels. Another must-see is the New Year’s Eve fireworks display – one of the biggest in the world.

Mardi Gras, USA
Mardi Gras falls on the day before Ash Wednesday, which marks the beginning of Lent in the Christian calendar. Also known as ‘Fat Tuesday’, its most famous celebration is hosted in the USA’s New Orleans – an explosion of colourful parades, elaborate costumes, jazz music, and street parties. Bourbon Street and The French Quarter see most of the action.

Festivals in India
India is a feast for the senses at any time of year, but even more so when its festivals are in full swing. There are many noteworthy ones, but some of the best include Diwali, which falls in October or November and celebrates light over darkness, the spring colour festival of Holi, with its dancing and paint throwing, and Eid, which marks the end of Ramadan with feasts and prayers.
Take me there
 
 
Festivals in Mexico
Mexican festivals are Technicolour treats of culture, history, and tradition. November’s Día de los Muertos (Day of the Dead) honours loved ones with altars and parades, while July’s Guelaguetza in Oaxaca showcases impressive indigenous dancing. Mexico’s Independence Day and Carnival bring lively parades, fireworks, and a deep sense of community pride, too.

Cherry Blossom Festivals, Japan
The Japan Cherry Blossom Festival, otherwise known as Hanami, is a candy coloured spring celebration of the fleeting beauty of sakura (cherry blossom trees). Locals and visitors come together under blossoming trees for picnics, enjoying traditional food, music, and dancing. One of the best places to see the spectacle is Kyoto, Japan’s ancient capital city.
