Experience is…embracing Japan’s rich culture
 
Intricate Shinto shrines, authentic ryokan inns, historic battle sites – Japan’s got it all. Visit Kanazawa to see traditional kimono displays and tour sake factories. Stopover in Osaka to explore the samurai district. Stroll through the UNESCO-listed mountain village of Shirakawa-go. And don’t miss the National Museum in Tokyo, to learn about all the above through the ages.

Experience is…discovering Japan’s natural wonders
 
Japan’s famous cherry blossom season falls in spring, with the ‘sakura’ trees usually blooming in late March and early April, although it varies across the country. There’s never a bad time to explore the beauty spots here, though. Lots of Japan is mountainous, and the countryside is made up of rice fields and woodlands. There are 34 national parks, the most popular being Fuji-Hakone-Izu National Park – home to Mount Fuji, Japan’s tallest peak.

Experience is…sightseeing in Japanese cosmopolitan capital
 
Japan’s capital, Tokyo needs little introduction. It’s the world’s most populated city, and a neon-lit mega-mix of futuristic skyscrapers, rainbow-hued shopfronts, and quiet, outer neighbourhoods lined with modest lantern-lit homes. Tokyo has an incredibly efficient subway system, which makes getting from A to B a breeze, too.

Experience is…trying Japanese cuisine
 
Japanese cuisine is famous all over the world, and for good reason. Net-fresh sushi, steaming bowls of ramen, and silky gyoza are menu mainstays, and Tokyo is home to more Michelin-starred restaurants than any other city. Fine dining is just one side of the story, though. Hit the backstreets and sample specialties like takoyaki – balls of octopus seasoned with ginger and pickled onion, and dorayaki, Japan’s take on fluffy American pancakes.
