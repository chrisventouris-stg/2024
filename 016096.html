  Experience is… perfectly planned, expertly led adventures
 
Our Grand Tour of Australia kicks off in the capital of Western Australia and Australia’s sunniest capital city: Perth. With weather as welcoming as its friendly locals, it’s the perfect place to start our adventure. The modern city, nestled in nature offers a taste of everything we love about the Land Down Under. There’s exquisite food and wine, native wildlife, 1‍9 pristine beaches, intimate cafes, dreamy Indian Ocean sunsets and one of the world’s largest inner city parks. Explore the thriving metropolis of curated cultural experiences, from indigenous art to exhibitions at UNESCO-listed Fremantle Prison.
We spend three nights here, so there’s plenty of time to get to know the vibrant city at a leisurely pace. Guided tours of Perth and Fremantle are included, and there’s an optional trip out to the nature reserve of Rottnest Island for anyone who fancies meeting adorable quokkas (small, marsupials) and exploring its 6‍3 spectacular beaches. We chose not to include lunch or dinner here, as there are countless great restaurants to try (ask your tour manager if you’d like some recommendations).
It's just the beginning of an amazing month of incredible experiences and unforgettable sights. Scroll down to see what else is in store on our Grand Tour…

  Grand Tour of Australia
 
 
2‍9 n‍ights from £8,‍8‍8‍5pp
From Western Australia’s sunny and vibrant capital city of Perth, we’ll travel north, to the tropical ‘Top End’ of Australia. Highlights here include Kakadu National Park and the gloriously rugged scenery along the Katherine River. The Red Centre is up next, where we’ll watch both sunset and sunrise in front of Uluru’s iconic silhouette.
The east coast follows. From Cairns, you can snorkel on the Great Barrier Reef and journey through the world’s oldest rainforest; at Noosa, you’ll sip sparkling wine as you boat through everglades and perhaps take a trip to Fraser Island. There’ll be koalas and kangaroos in Brisbane, wine tasting in Hunter Valley and a seafood lunch while we cruise the waters of Sydney Harbour.
The tour finishes up in Melbourne. Travel through eucalyptus forests on a restored steam train, pitstop at Yarra Valley wineries for tastings, and maybe head out to Phillip Island to see the adorable fairy penguins.

  Getting there
 
 
It’s a long way to Australia, so you want to know you’ll be travelling in comfort. Our Grand Tour includes flights both ways with Qantas, Australia’s flagship carrier. From the minute you step on board, you’ll enjoy friendly Aussie service, spacious cabins, locally inspired cuisine and self-service snack bars. The in-flight entertainment is excellent too, so you can catch up on a few blockbusters or binge a box set or two while you’re in the air.
