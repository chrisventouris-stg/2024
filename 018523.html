Experience is…exploring Western Australia’s captivating cities
Situated on the banks of the Swan River, Perth is a modern city that’s nestled in nature. Australia’s sunniest capital is surrounded by incredible wineries, one of the world’s largest inner-city parks and 19 spectacular Indian Ocean beaches. Stroll along the Swan River and you’ll be treated to countless art galleries, charming café culture, fresh local seafood, and the world-class Crown Theatre. Head south and the historic and bohemian port city of Freemantle is worth exploring, as is Mandurah – which overlooks canals of dolphins. Australia’s most premium wine region Margaret River has impressive wineries, an otherworldly coastline, gourmet cuisine and rich wildlife.

Experience is…encounters with nature and wildlife
Western Australia has 112 picturesque national parks, eight of which are within two hours’ drive of Perth city. Along with countless walking and hiking trails, you’ll encounter creatures like adorable quokkas (small, smiling marsupials that are endemic to the nature reserve of Rottnest Island), echidnas, eagles, and rare black-footed wallabies on land, while turtles, dolphins, manta rays, humpback whales and majestic whale sharks can be found in the surrounding waters. You can swim with them at the UNESCO-listed Nigaloo Reef.
Find out more
 
 
Experience is…feeling the sand between your toes
Western Australia is blessed with boundless beautiful beaches. You’ve got snow-white stretches of sand in the likes of Perth, Esperance and Margaret River, and castaway island escapes off the coast, such as dreamy Abrolhos and Rottnest. This is also prime snorkelling and scuba diving territory, thanks to multiple shipwrecks and coral reefs like Ningaloo (the world’s largest fringing reef) – home to 200 species of colourful coral and more than 500 species of rainbow-hued fish.
