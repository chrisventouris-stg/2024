Japan is a country of captivating contrasts – where centuries-old temples sit quietly beneath the glow of neon signs, and ancient traditions thrive alongside cutting-edge innovation. One moment you could be strolling through Kyoto’s peaceful gardens, the next gazing up at Tokyo’s skyscraper-filled skyline. It’s a place that captures the imagination – and stays with you long after you’ve left.
Japan’s unfamiliar language and culture may feel a little daunting to some, but that’s where Saga comes in. With an expert tour manager by your side on our escorted group tours, you’ll be fully supported every step of the way. Whether it’s navigating a local menu or unlocking the meaning behind a centuries-old ritual, we’ll help you experience Japan with confidence and ease. Alternatively, we offer independent itineraries (still featuring knowledgeable local guides) if you want to explore under your own steam.
Depending on the tour you choose, you could spend a night in a traditional ryokan, take in views of Mount Fuji framed by cherry blossoms, zoom across the country on a bullet train, or watch snow monkeys soaking in natural hot springs. Each itinerary offers a different way to experience Japan’s culture and beauty – but which one tempts you most?
